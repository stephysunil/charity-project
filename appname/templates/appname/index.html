Why Choose Life & Limb?

Life and Limb is a charity focused on providing prosthetic limbs and support to amputees. The organization aims to restore mobility and independence for individuals who have lost limbs due to injury, illness, or congenital conditions. By offering access to high-quality prosthetics and rehabilitation services, Life and Limb helps improve the quality of life for its recipients. Additionally, the charity often engages in advocacy and awareness efforts to highlight the needs of amputees and promote inclusivity in society.

Prosthetics and Orthotics

Specialists who design, create, and fit artificial limbs tailored to the individual’s needs and lifestyle.

Comprehensive assessment of the patient’s needs, goals, and the condition of the residual limb. Creating tailored prosthetic limbs based on individual measurements, functional requirements, and aesthetic preferences.Ensuring that the prosthetic limb fits properly and comfortably, making adjustments as necessary



Psychology/Psychiatry

Addresses the psychological aspects of limb loss and adjustment to using a prosthetic limb, providing emotional support.

Assessing the patient’s mental health status, coping mechanisms, and emotional responses related to limb loss.Providing individual therapy to help patients process their feelings about limb loss, including grief, anxiety, and depression.Supporting patients in adjusting to their new reality, including changes in body image and identity.



Occupational Therapy

Occupational therapists assist patients in adapting to daily activities and tasks using their artificial limb, promoting independence.

Evaluating the patient’s abilities, limitations, and needs related to activities of daily living (ADLs) and occupational roles. Developing individualized treatment plans that focus on enhancing the patient’s functional abilities and promoting independence.



Social Work

Social workers may provide support in navigating insurance, resources, and emotional support for patients and families.

Organizing and facilitating support groups for patients and families can provide a platform for shared experiences and coping strategies. These groups help reduce isolation and promote community among those affected by limb loss.
